About me

I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Sociology from the University of Buenos Aires (UBA). I originally began my studies in Economics at the same university, but later decided to shift my focus toward the social sciences driven by an academic curiosity. I was drawn to the dynamic and ever-evolving nature of sociology, with its multiple paradigms and its vast body of knowledge that continuously grows through the contributions of many. I felt compelled to grow in a field where there was still so much to explore, many unanswered questions and, naturally—if I may—much to learn and grasp.

I graduated in 2018 and have since built extensive experience in both the private and public sectors. I worked for six years in the private sector, where I gained valuable skills and applied knowledge related to scientific research in the field of social sciences. At the same time, I dedicated around 15 years to public sector research, primarily focused on History as an academic discipline in the City of Buenos Aires, Argentina.

I also work in website creation, which I approach as the design of virtual experiences. My focus is on entrepreneurs of all kinds—businesses, local shops, professionals, freelancers, and creatives—providing them with a platform to express themselves and showcase their services, products, and potential.

Beyond my academic background in Sociology, I develop projects across diverse fields—from manual and artisanal work to editing, video production, image design, and scriptwriting for social media. In addition, I manage a private urban lodging venture in Buenos Aires.
