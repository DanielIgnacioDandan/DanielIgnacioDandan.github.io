About me

I hold a Bachelor’s Degree in Sociology from the University of Buenos Aires (UBA). Initially, I embarked on my academic journey in Economic Sciences at the same institution, but I chose to redirect my focus towards the social sciences driven by a profound intellectual curiosity. The dynamic and continually evolving nature of sociology, with its diverse paradigms and extensive body of knowledge that is perpetually enhanced by numerous contributions, captivated me. I found it to be a field rich with opportunities for discovery and learning.

I graduated in 2018 and have since amassed a wealth of experience in both the private and public sectors. Over the course of six years, I gained valuable skills and knowledge in the private sector, applicable across various fields. Subsequently, I dedicated approximately 15 years to the public sector, working in two notable institutions. At the Parque de la Memoria, I spent 15 years focusing on issues related to the disappeared and murdered during Argentina’s State Terrorism. Additionally, I spent seven months at the Museo Luis Perlotti, where I conducted research on pre-Columbian identities in Argentina and their mechanisms of survival and adaptation to the present day.

Throughout my studies and career, I have encountered more questions as I found answers, with each question serving as an opportunity to deepen and enrich my understanding. From the Selk'nam in Tierra del Fuego to the Coyas and Diaguitas in the Northwest, and the Guaraní in the Northeast, through the Viceroyalties of the Río de la Plata and Upper Peru, up to the establishment of the Argentine Republic at the end of the 19th century, my interest in diverse cultures and their evolution has been a constant. This has involved a perspective that encompasses local, regional, and global dialogues.

In addition to my background in Sociology, I engage in a range of versatile fields, from artisanal manual work to video editing and web development. I also manage a private guest accommodation business and believe there are always new techniques and trades to learn. This mindset allows me to adapt to various challenges and opportunities.
